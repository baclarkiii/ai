Part I Multiple Choice (Questions 1-5)

Select the single best response for each of the following conceptual and analytical questions.

1. Two point charges, $q_1 = +2.0\,\mu\text{C}$ and $q_2 = -6.0\,\mu\text{C}$, are separated by a distance $r$. If the electrostatic force exerted on $q_1$ by $q_2$ is $\vec{F}_{12}$, which of the following statements regarding the electrostatic force $\vec{F}_{21}$ exerted on $q_2$ by $q_1$ is correct?

2. Two identical metallic spheres on insulating stands carry charges of $+8.0\,\mu\text{C}$ and $-2.0\,\mu\text{C}$ respectively. They are brought into brief contact, allowed to share charge, and then returned to their original separation distance $d$. What is the ratio of the new electrostatic force magnitude to the original electrostatic force magnitude?

3. The permittivity of free space ($\varepsilon_0$) is a fundamental physical constant. If the media separating two charges is switched from a vacuum to a liquid with a relative permittivity (dielectric constant) of $\kappa = 4.0$, how does the magnitude of the electrostatic force between the point charges change?

4. A point charge $q_1 = +4.0\,\mu\text{C}$ is placed at the origin $(0,0)$, and a second point charge $q_2 = +9.0\,\mu\text{C}$ is placed on the x-axis at $x = 1.0\text{ m}$. At what coordinate position $x$ on the x-axis can a third charge $q_3 = -1.0\,\mu\text{C}$ be positioned such that the net electrostatic force acting on it is exactly zero?

5. The force-distance curve representing Coulomb's Law corresponds to which mathematical function?

Part II Fill in the Blank (Questions 6-8)

Provide precise, rounded numerical values or short-text answers. Include appropriate metric units where indicated.

6. Two helium nuclei (alpha particles), each containing exactly 2 protons (charge $e = 1.60 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$), are separated by a distance of $3.00 \times 10^{-15}\text{ m}$ inside a nucleus. Using $k_e = 8.99 \times 10^9\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$, the repulsive electrostatic force acting between them is Newtons. (Round answer to the nearest whole integer).
7. When Coulomb's Law is written in vector form, the unit vector $\hat{r}_{12}$ designates the of the force vector projecting from charge 1 towards charge 2. (Enter a single word, e.g., 'direction', 'slope', or 'magnitude').
8. Two objects are charged by rubbing them together. If one object acquires a net positive charge of $+4.8 \times 10^{-14}\text{ C}$, the other object must have gained electrons. (Provide an exact integer count based on the charge of a single electron).
