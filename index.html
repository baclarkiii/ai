Biography

Dr. Beverly Clark III is the Vice President for Academic Affairs and a Tenured Professor of Physics. Throughout a career spanning over two decades, Dr. Clark has blended the complexities of institutional leadership with an active dedication to physics education and scientific inquiry.

Under his leadership as VPAA, the university has successfully modernized its curriculum, introduced innovative hybrid learning paradigms, and significantly increased funding for undergraduate student research programs. As a researcher, his primary interest lies in computational electromagnetic modeling, wave propagation, and innovative pedagogical tools that make hard sciences approachable to all students.

Teaching Philosophy & Courses

"Physics is best understood not through memory, but through direct observation and modeling." Dr. Clark prioritizes active learning paradigms and provides students with interactive computational simulations to visualize complex, unseen forces.

PHY-301: Advanced Electrodynamics

Exploration of Maxwell's Equations, electromagnetic radiation, and vector calculus applications.

PHY-410: Classical & Wave Mechanics

Advanced mathematical applications, Lagrangian mechanics, and wave equation parameters.
